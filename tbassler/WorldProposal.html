WorldFactory

*This remains unchanged from the handed-in paper in class*

The world would generate randomly shaped and sized clumps of elements.  Herds would consist of an initial animal (leader) placed via randomly generated coordinates, then each of the 8 adjacent bricks would have a chance of containing a following member of the heard, and for each follower, around them is a lower chance follower until all followers have had a chance to have more followers around them.  This will create random shaped groupings with a strong bias to group near the leader.  Static elements would be generated through a similar algorithm, but only along the nearest 4 bricks with a higher starting chance to spread, with a similar drop-off rate.  Each kind of entity will be placed at random, type selected at the same time as coordinates.  Naturally, if there is already an entity in a brick, the placement for that brick will be skipped.  This should create a fairly natural looking pattern of entities with a graceful and compact codebase.
